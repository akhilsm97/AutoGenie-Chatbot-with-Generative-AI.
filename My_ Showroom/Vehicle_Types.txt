?Compact size

?Good mileage

?Ideal for city driving

Common Vehicle Components

1. Engine

Definition:

The powerhouse that converts fuel into mechanical energy.

Types: Petrol, Diesel, Hybrid, Electric


2. Transmission

Definition:

System that transmits engine power to the wheels.

Types: Manual, Automatic, CVT, Dual-clutch


3. Brakes

Definition:

Used to slow down or stop the vehicle.

Types: Disc brakes, Drum brakes, ABS (Anti-lock Braking System)


4. Suspension

Definition:

Provides stability and absorbs road shocks.

Parts: Shock absorbers, springs, struts


5. Battery

Definition:

Provides electrical power to start the engine and run accessories.

Types: Lead-acid (traditional), Lithium-ion (EVs)


Common Problems

1. Engine Overheating

Causes:

?Low coolant

?Faulty thermostat

?Radiator issues

Solutions:

?Check coolant levels

?Flush radiator

?Replace faulty components


2. Brake Noise or Failure

Causes:

?Worn brake pads

?Damaged rotor

?Low brake fluid

Solutions:

?Replace brake pads

?Inspect brake fluid

?Check rotor condition


3. Battery Drain

Causes:

?Old battery

?Parasitic draw

?Alternator issue

Solutions:

?Battery replacement

?Electrical check

?Alternator test


4. Transmission Slipping

Causes:

?Low transmission fluid

?Worn clutch (manual)

?Transmission wear

Solutions:

?Refill/flush transmission fluid

?Inspect clutch

?Transmission rebuild


Diagnostic Tests

1. OBD Scan (On-Board Diagnostics)

Purpose:

Detect engine and system faults via scanner.

Tests For:

?Check engine light

?Emissions

?Sensor status


2. Wheel Alignment Test

Purpose

Ensures wheels are aligned properly for safety and tire longevity.

Indications:

?Vehicle pulling to one side

?Uneven tire wear


3. Brake Inspection

Purpose:

Checks brake pad thickness, fluid levels, and rotor condition.

Frequency:

Every 6 months or 10,000 km

4. Emission Test

Purpose:

Measures pollutants in exhaust gases.

Required For:

Registration renewals (in many countries)

Environmental compliance
